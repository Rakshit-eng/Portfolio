⭐ Featured Project 🔬 Materials Research 🌿 Sustainability 🏗️ Zero Energy Buildings
Development of a Bio-Composite Smart Capillary Matrix for Passive Evaporative Cooling in Zero Energy Buildings
A novel materials engineering approach to eliminating mechanical cooling dependency in buildings — entirely passively.
Research in Progress
PROJECT OVERVIEW

This research project addresses one of the most pressing challenges in modern sustainable architecture: reducing the energy consumption of buildings without compromising thermal comfort. Globally, buildings account for approximately 40% of total energy use, with HVAC (heating, ventilation, and air conditioning) systems consuming the lion's share. This project proposes an entirely passive, material-based alternative — a Bio-Composite Smart Capillary Matrix (BSCM) — engineered to harness evaporative cooling physics at the microscale.

THE PROBLEM

Conventional air conditioning systems are energy-intensive, refrigerant-dependent, and contribute to the urban heat island effect — the very problem they are trying to solve at the individual scale. In a country like India, where ambient temperatures routinely exceed 40°C and electricity grids are under consistent stress, passive cooling is not just desirable — it is essential. Existing passive solutions (cool roofs, green walls, phase-change materials) either lack adaptability, require significant civil integration, or fail under low-humidity conditions.

THE PROPOSED SOLUTION

The Bio-Composite Smart Capillary Matrix is a multi-layered, biologically-inspired material assembly designed to autonomously regulate surface evaporation based on ambient humidity and temperature. The matrix integrates: natural bio-composite fibers (derived from sustainable sources such as jute, coir, and cellulose-based aerogels) as the primary capillary medium; a smart hygroscopic coating that expands capillary pore geometry under high thermal load, increasing evaporative surface area automatically; and a passive wicking network inspired by transpiration vascular structures in plants, enabling self-regulating water distribution without pumps or sensors.

🌿
Bio-Composite Fibers
Natural fibers (jute, coir, cellulose aerogels) selected for optimal capillary rise height, hydrophilicity, and structural integrity under cyclic wetting.
💧
Smart Capillary Network
Biomimetic vascular channel design inspired by plant xylem enables passive, pressure-driven water distribution without mechanical components.
🌡️
Hygroscopic Adaptive Coating
A responsive polymer coating that dynamically modifies pore diameter in response to temperature gradient, optimizing evaporation rate autonomously.
🏢
Zero Energy Building Integration
Designed as a retrofit-compatible panel system for exterior walls and rooftops — deployable without structural modification to existing buildings.
RESEARCH METHODOLOGY & PHASES
01
Literature Review & Material Selection
Comprehensive review of evaporative cooling thermodynamics, capillary rise phenomena (Washburn equation), and existing bio-composite material studies. Selection criteria include capillary rise height, tensile wet strength, biodegradability, and thermal conductivity.
02
Matrix Fabrication & Composite Preparation
Fabrication of prototype BSCM panels using layered fiber assembly, resin infiltration under vacuum, and application of hygroscopic coating via dip-coating and sol-gel techniques. Multiple formulations tested for structural and functional optimization.
03
Thermal & Evaporative Performance Testing
Laboratory-scale testing of surface temperature reduction, evaporation rate (g/m²/hr), and latent heat flux under controlled thermal load conditions. Comparison against baseline (uncoated concrete) and commercial cool roof surfaces.
04
Humidity-Response & Adaptive Behavior Analysis
Characterization of the smart coating's responsiveness across a range of relative humidity (20%–90%) and temperature (25°C–50°C) conditions. SEM imaging for pore geometry analysis pre and post actuation.
05
Energy Savings Modelling & ZEB Integration Assessment
Simulation of building-scale cooling load reduction using EnergyPlus/TRNSYS modelling. Estimation of annual kWh savings, CO₂ offset, and water consumption efficiency for a typical Indian residential building in a hot-dry climate zone.
KEY INNOVATION

What distinguishes this project from conventional evaporative coolers or wet-wall systems is the intelligence embedded in the material itself. There are no sensors, no actuators, no control systems — the matrix responds to environmental stimuli autonomously through its engineered microstructure. This makes it inherently reliable, maintenance-free, and manufacturable at low cost using locally available agricultural by-products, making it particularly suited for deployment across rural and semi-urban India where energy infrastructure is limited.

⚡ Electronics · Circuit Design
Automatic Door Operating Circuit

Designed and implemented an automatic door actuation system using IR proximity sensing and relay-based motor control circuitry. The circuit detects human presence within a defined threshold range and triggers a DC motor driver to open and close a door mechanism — entirely without manual intervention.

The project involved schematic design, component selection (IR emitter/detector pair, NE555 timer, L293D motor driver), breadboard prototyping, and PCB layout planning. Key challenges addressed included debounce logic, false-trigger suppression under ambient IR interference, and motor braking for precise door positioning.
